Edura
A platform for learning

Technology

Edura is a web application based on open edX

OPEN edX

Open edX is the open-source platform software developed by American online education actor EdX and made freely available to other institutions of higher learning that want to make similar offerings.

ABOUT

Online courses have a high drop-off rate, with 30% of students dropping out in week 1 and week 2. Only around 32% of people finish a course. Our mission is to curb this drop-off rate by our self-pace learning platform Edura

  • Data Driven Decision Making

    By allowing content poviders to quickly access consumption charts and information in one place they decisions can be more informed and data driven

  • Quick access to relevant student history

    Currently content providers do not have a way to understand their audience. We collet and aggregate multiple sources, to form a learning profile of the content consumers. With Edura all this information is part of the same system

Sara Eriksson

My main contribution to this project was research and user testing with the doctors, to inform the design process. I also made the video submission for C-awards. I prepared the user story for the final presentation, and the presentation itself. I did some work on the HTML and CSS of the website (that we used to test with the visualization with the doctors) until we decided to use React, where I only contributed with some changes to the design.

  • Preliminary research ~
  • User testing and documentation ~ 10 hours
  • Video submission ~
  • Final Presentation ~ 3 hours
  • Design process ~5 hours
  • Internal meetings ~5 hours

We set up a slack channel where we communicated within the group and our partners at Karolinska and Jansen & Jansen. It did take me some time to remember to check slack everyday, because I can’t have the app on my phone, so my team mates had to remind me through Facebook messenger in the beginning of the project. Each of us did responsibly communicate what we have been working on and what we need from the others via the Slack Channel.

There were some ideas that arose from the user studies and research that did not make it to the final cut for several reasons - i.e. time frame of the project, not valuable to the user etc.

What I have learned in this project is that team and project management is the most important thing when working in a large group, a skill I need to practice more. Also the importance of understanding the user’s environment, practice and needs - which is also why I love HCI. We spent a lot of time in this project understanding the terms (PSA-value, Gleeson-score, comorbidities), how they are used in practice and how the doctors make sense of the all the data today, to be able create something that can support their cognition. The importance to be able to kill your darlings in behalf of the user’s needs. Further, I have gained some insight into React.

Mohit Ahuja

I worked in various phases of this project, from the design to the development. From planning to the onsite deployment, from working on code to meeting the users for user testing in later phasess. I also contributed by documenting our team meetings.   • Visualization Research ~2 hours
  • User testing and documentation ~3 hours
  • Backend ~6 hours
  • Project and Resource Management ~8 hours
  • Visualization of NPCR data ~10 hours
  • Design process ~3 hours
  • Internal meetings ~5 hours
  • Onsite deployment ~6 hours
  • Presentation ~ 3 hours

I am probably the most active member on slack, as I feel that communication is one of the core components of a team. I have cherished working with people on this team, as they were good at taking responsibilities at various phases of the project.

I worked on getting the backend and database right, as it should be done according to the best practices, unfortunately it did not make the cut to final version of the app, the time frame was small for this type of work to be done.

I learned that there are times when you need to manage the project individually and sometimes at the macro level. The key take away from this project is how to manage a lot of moving parts and make sure to keep moving things in the moving direction. In later stages I also realized again the very core tenet of software development(also applicable to information visualization some would say): Make it WORK, then Make it RIGHT, then Make it FAST.

Mustafa Saeed

I mostly worked on the development part of the project. I was the one to propose we setup the project using React Js for the core purpose of maintaining a responsive and clean interface for developing the platform. I took part in the initial setup and the worked with the team to get up to speed with the platform. I also took part in the initial phase of the design and feedback loops to understand the requirements.

  • User testing and documentation ~2 hours
  • Backend ~1 hours
  • Project Coding and Project Management ~ 21 hours
  • Visualization of NPCR data ~5 hours
  • Design process ~6 hours
  • Internal meetings ~ 5 hours
  • Onsite deployment ~ 8 hours
  • Presentation ~ 1 hours

The team communication was made using Slack that we also connected with the team at KI. We scheduled meetings and the if required necessary communication over the platform.

Initial design of the charts based on the feedback from the doctors we designed a bubble chart model to distinguish patient details. We later decided based feedback that we should aim for simple charts for clear decision making.

Other than the knowledge about prostate cancer that was eye opening literally I was able to gather requirements, clean data, produce visualizations that were meaningful and the realizations that generating simple visualizations even though seems like an ordinary task but it is actually the most difficult to achieve the right balance between meaningful and flashy visualizations.

Fanny Chan

I mostly contributed in user testing, coordinating meetings with doctors and developing the visualization of NPCR data. Although the visualization with donut charts were relatively easy to develop, we spent quite a lot of time on it because we changed the design rather late in the project since we realized that the previous design was not understandable for the doctors. In the beginning I was also part of designing the overall design of the website, then we changed to react and I did not participate in the design process anymore.

  • User testing and documentation ~10 hours
  • Coordinating meetings ~1 hours
  • Visualization of NPCR data ~10 hours
  • Design process ~5 hours
  • Internal meetings ~5 hours
  • Proposal presentation ~

All group communication was through our Slack channel. When contacting specific persons, we tried to directly contact that person through Slack or preferred way, e.g. Facebook messenger.

In the donut chart we were supposed to be able to change the values in order to see how the probability of death would change depending of different factors, e.g. if you were in the age between 60-60 or 50-59 at diagnosis. However, in the current design, only the probability of the current patient will be shown and it is not possible to change.

Since our project is very much about to create something that was suitable for the doctors and the context of use, therefore I learnt a lot about user-centered design. But I also learnt to work in bigger groups and about React. .

Robin Setterwall

We divided up the various coding parts of the project early on. I was in charge of working on the medication chart. This took way longer than I anticipated since there were problems with the integration of the code onto the server.

  • Design ~ 3 hours
  • Coding (front-end) ~ 25 hours
  • Presentation ~ 2 hours

I found it difficult to keep up with the communication in the general slack channel. I’ve never used it before but it was highly praised by the rest of the team. I can understand why we wouldn’t want to communicate through Facebook Messenger, there is too much activity there already. In retrospect I wish we had divided the team into smaller parts and eventually connect up the pieces and finalise the project. A shared Google Calendar could’ve been a good addition as well, it was pretty difficult to find a good time slot for everyone to meet up.

I worked on the Gantt chart (medication chart). The first version of it wasn’t compatible with react which we decided on using a while into the project, so it was scrapped. The second version took some time writing up but the real trouble with this one was, again, making it work with the server. The good aspect of react is that it is easy to update values in elements, this wasn’t a huge benefit to the medication chart but in turn caused a lot of problems for me in the implementation phase.

Coding in a new language is difficult. Balancing the time between all the different courses is very difficult. What I’ve learned from this project is that I need to be more on point with my communication with the rest of the team. I enjoyed working with this team and I’ve learned much from them, I’ve gained insight into technologies I haven’t used before but also design, evaluation, presentation and team management. The most valuable thing I took from this project however was the importance of time and team management, it has improved during these past few months but I still have much to learn!
